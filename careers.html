Career Clusters

Finance & Banking

What they do: Manage money, advise clients, raise capital, analyze investments.

Qualifications: Bachelor’s in Finance, Economics, Accounting. Skills: Excel, financial modeling, networking.

Top Universities: UPenn (Wharton), Harvard, NYU Stern

Salary Progression: Analyst $70–$120K → Associate $120–$180K → VP/Director $200–$400K → MD/Partner $500K+.

Management Consulting

What they do: Advise companies on strategy, operations, and efficiency improvements.

Qualifications: Degree in Finance, Business, Economics. Skills: problem-solving, communication, analytics.

Top Universities: Harvard, Stanford, Wharton

Salary Progression: Analyst $95–$120K → Consultant $130–$180K → Manager $200–$250K → Partner $400K+.

Technology & Software

What they do: Develop software, maintain systems, design applications, innovate tech solutions.

Qualifications: CS or Engineering degree. Skills: coding (Python, Java, etc.), teamwork, problem-solving.

Top Universities: MIT, Stanford, UC Berkeley

Salary Progression: Entry $80–$120K → Mid $120–$160K → Senior $160–$220K → Lead/Manager $200K+.

Healthcare & Medicine

What they do: Diagnose, treat, and prevent illnesses. Includes doctors, nurses, and specialists.

Qualifications: MD/DO degree, residency, board certification. Skills: empathy, analysis, medical knowledge.

Top Universities: Johns Hopkins, Mayo Clinic School of Medicine, Harvard Medical School

Salary Progression: Resident $60–$70K → Attending Physician $200–$400K+ → Specialist $300–$500K+.

Law & Legal Services

What they do: Represent clients, draft documents, provide legal advice, handle court cases.

Qualifications: JD degree, pass bar exam, law firm internships. Skills: research, argumentation, critical thinking.

Top Universities: Yale, Harvard, Stanford

Salary Progression: Associate $120–$150K → Senior Associate $160–$200K → Partner $300K+.

Engineering & Manufacturing

What they do: Design, build, and improve products, machines, and systems in various industries.

Qualifications: Bachelor’s in Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil). Skills: CAD, problem-solving, math.

Top Universities: MIT, Stanford, Georgia Tech

Salary Progression: Entry $70–$90K → Mid $100–$130K → Senior $150–$180K → Lead/Manager $180–$220K+

Entrepreneurship & Startups

What they do: Launch and manage businesses, develop products, raise funds, lead teams.

Qualifications: Business or Finance degree (optional), leadership skills, networking, risk management.

Top Universities: Stanford, Harvard, UPenn (Wharton)

Salary Progression: Highly variable – depends on business success; founders can earn $0 → millions.
