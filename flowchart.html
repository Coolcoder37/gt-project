My Career & Business Plan

This flowchart shows my plan to major in Finance and pursue a career as a Management Consultant, or alternatively to start my own factory business. Each branch illustrates the steps, skills, education, and experiences I want to gain to achieve my long-term goals.

Consulting Path
High School: Focus on Math & Finance Clubs
Bachelor's: Major in Finance at a Top University
Internships: Gain experience in consulting & finance
MBA: Top Business School for Strategic Management
Entry-Level Consultant: Work at a top consulting firm (e.g., McKinsey, BCG)
Career Growth: Senior Consultant → Manager → Partner
2ND CHOICE: CEO / Business Builder Path
High School (Grades 10–12): Learn sales, basic business, and operations by doing small B2B service projects (Excel cleanup, workflows, automation)
High School: Cold call / email local businesses, identify inefficiencies, sell solutions, and outsource work to freelancers per project
High School: Earn first real revenue ($500–$5,000 total), build portfolio, manage freelancers, and prove entrepreneurship to colleges
College (Years 1–2): Focus on B2B clients in logistics, warehouses, and small manufacturing; sell higher-value operational and automation solutions
College (Years 1–2): Act as CEO and project manager — sell, scope projects, collect upfront payments, and outsource execution
College (Years 2–3): Transition into small physical systems (custom racks, conveyor add-ons, semi-automated workstations)
College (Years 2–3): Pre-sell projects, manage designers and builders per job, and generate consistent revenue
Post-College / Late College: Scale into full custom conveyor and automation systems with repeat clients
Growth Phase: Reinvest profits, standardize designs, reduce outsourcing, and increase margins
Factory Stage: Open your own warehouse/factory to build systems in-house, hire employees, and scale to high revenue
